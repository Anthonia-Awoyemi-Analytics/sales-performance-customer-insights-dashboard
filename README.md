# Sales Performance Analysis Dashboard

A business intelligence dashboard project focused on analyzing sales performance, customer behavior, product contribution, and operational reporting visibility using Excel, PostgreSQL, and Power BI.

This project was developed to transform fragmented operational sales records into a structured reporting system capable of supporting clearer business monitoring and more informed decision making.

# Business Problem

The business generated large volumes of operational sales data, but much of the reporting process remained manual and fragmented across spreadsheets and transactional records. This made it difficult to monitor revenue trends consistently, identify high performing products and customers quickly, or gain a clearer understanding of overall sales performance.

The goal of this project was to develop a structured and interactive reporting solution that could improve visibility into sales trends, customer contribution behavior, product performance, and operational activity.

Rather than focusing only on visualization, I approached the project as a practical business intelligence solution intended to support faster operational understanding and more informed business decision making.

⸻

# Tools & Technologies

# Dashboard Overview

The dashboard was intentionally divided into three focused analytical sections to make the reporting experience more structured and easier to navigate.

⸻

# Executive Overview

Focused on high-level KPI monitoring, monthly revenue trends, product contribution analysis, and top customer performance visibility. This page was designed to give decision makers a quick understanding of overall business performance without overwhelming them with excessive detail.

⸻

# Product Analysis

Explored how products contributed differently to operational activity and revenue generation. The analysis revealed strong concentration around rope related product categories and highlighted important differences between high volume and high value product performance.

⸻

# Customer Analysis

Focused on customer purchasing behavior, revenue contribution, and customer value patterns. One of the key findings from this section was that customers driving the highest sales quantities were not always the customers generating the strongest revenue contribution.

⸻

# Key Insights

Rope related products consistently dominated overall sales contribution and operational activity.

High sales quantity did not always correspond with the highest revenue contribution.

F.E Energy generated the highest sales quantity, while Peter Warri generated the strongest revenue contribution.

Some lower contributing customers showed inconsistent purchasing behavior, highlighting opportunities for deeper customer segmentation and retention analysis.

Structured reporting significantly improved visibility into monthly sales patterns and operational performance trends.

⸻

# Data Limitations

Some reporting periods within the dataset, particularly parts of early 2024, were not consistently documented in the available operational records. Because of this, the dashboard reflects the available recorded sales activity rather than a complete historical representation of all business transactions.

Instead of estimating missing periods, I focused on maintaining transparency and working with the documented operational data available.
